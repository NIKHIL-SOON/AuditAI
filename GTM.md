# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## Exact Target User
**Title**: VP of Engineering, CTO, or Engineering Manager 
**Company Stage**: Seed to Series B ($1M–$15M funding), 10–100 employees.

At this stage, the engineering team is large enough that rogue SaaS spend accumulates. Developers swipe corporate cards for Cursor, ChatGPT Plus, and Claude accounts without central oversight. Crucially, these startups lack a dedicated FinOps team. The technical leader is accountable for the burn rate but too busy to audit licenses via a spreadsheet.

## Trigger Moment
The ideal trigger is right after a monthly financial review. Finance asks, "Why is our software tooling bill up 40%?" or "We have 30 Copilot seats but only 22 engineers, why?" Alternatively, they might be scrolling Hacker News seeing a debate about "Cursor vs Copilot," wondering if they are paying a premium for outdated tools.

## Where They Hang Out
- **Hacker News**: The primary watering hole for technical leaders.
- **Subreddits**: r/SaaS, r/startups, and r/ExperiencedDevs.
- **Slack Communities**: Y Combinator alumni networks, Rands Leadership Slack, and CTO Craft.
- **X (Twitter)**: Engaging in "build in public" threads and engineering leadership circles.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Paid Budget)
We execute a targeted, value-first organic approach:

1. **The "Open Source Calculator" Launch (Show HN)**
Engineers are allergic to marketing but love auditing tools. I will launch on Hacker News framing the tool as a utility: *"Show HN: I built a tool that calculates exactly how much you overpay for Cursor/Claude based on actual usage limits."* This drives curious, high-intent traffic.

2. **Hyper-Personalized Cold Outreach**
I will export a Crunchbase list of companies that announced Seed/Series A rounds recently. I will cold email the CTOs: 
*"Hey [Name], noticing you're scaling the team. I built a free, no-login calculator that scans your AI dev tool stack and finds rightsizing savings. It saves teams your size about 15% immediately. Thought you might want to run your stack through it to keep burn down."*

3. **Private Founder Slack Drops**
I will leverage founder networks with a highly contextual offer: *"We built an AI spend auditor. If it finds >$1,000 in monthly savings, DM me the report and I'll personally help you migrate your API keys to get enterprise rates."*

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
The one thing only Credex can do is *instantly provision the savings*. Other companies are just auditors. Because Credex supplies discounted credits, our unfair advantage is that the audit isn't theoretical. We can say: "You overspend by $800 a month on Anthropic. Click here, and we will provision $10,000 in credits at a 20% discount right now." 

## Week-1 Traction Expectations
- **Traffic**: 3,000 unique visitors.
- **Audits Completed**: 450 (15% landing conversion).
- **Emails Captured**: 135 (30% capture rate).
- **High-Intent Leads**: 20 (leads with >$500/mo in savings).
